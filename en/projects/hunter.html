A ritual nobody enjoys

3

Push instead of pull

Three times a day, the bot scrapes hh.ru and Habr using your search queries. Requests use rate-limiting and rotation to avoid overloading sources. Every vacancy goes through personal scoring. In the morning, a digest arrives in Telegram — you don't visit job sites, job sites come to you.

Vacancies below 40 points are hidden. You only see what actually fits.

4

How scoring works

Each vacancy gets a 0–100 score based on four factors:

Skills — 40%. Weighted matching. The first skill in your list weighs more, the last — less. Psychology: what you type first is your strongest skill.

Salary — 25%. Range overlap detection. Currency normalization (USD, EUR → RUB).

Format — 20%. Remote, hybrid, office. Exact match — 100 points. Hybrid for a remote worker — 50.

Industry — 15%. 22 predefined domains with keyword sets.

Red flags in the description halve the score. Blacklisted company — zero.

5

Cover letters in one tap

Found an interesting vacancy — tap "Letter". Claude writes a cover letter based on the user's real experience and skills, tailored to the specific vacancy. No fabrication — only facts from the profile.

Don't like it — "Another version" generates a fresh one with a different structure.

Each letter takes 10–15 seconds instead of 10–15 minutes.

6

14 steps to your first digest

On start, the bot walks you through onboarding: name, job title, experience, skills, salary range, format, industries, red flags, company blacklist, search queries, portfolio, experience description.

Every step uses inline buttons. You can skip, go back, edit later. Right after completion — your first scrape and digest.

Freemium

Hybrid model: subscription for active users, credits for those who just need one or two letters.
