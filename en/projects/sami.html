Pet project
1

Not motivation. Structure.

People want to move. They open YouTube — ten million videos. They browse for twenty minutes, close it, turn on Netflix.

The problem isn't laziness. The problem is that every day you need to decide again: what to do, how long, what level.

Sami is a Telegram channel that removes this choice. One workout per day. Just a mat. Stretching, strength, mobility, yoga, breathing, cardio, recovery — seven categories, seven days.

3

Seasons

One season is 21 days. Three full weeks. Each day is tied to its category: Monday — stretching, Tuesday — strength, Wednesday — mobility.

It's a ritual, not a program. No need to plan. Open the channel — do it — close it.

After a workout you can tap "I did it" in the discussion. The bot counts your streak and shows the total. No rankings, no competition — just a record for yourself.

4

Where content comes from

The bot searches YouTube for workouts using curated keywords. Every video goes through a scoring algorithm: views, likes, channel authority, duration.

But the main filter is brand values. 70+ patterns reject content with "fix your body" rhetoric, weight loss focus, or competitive framing. Only calm, instructional bodyweight videos pass through.

YouTube is restricted in Russia, so the bot downloads the video, validates the codec (H.264 for Telegram), transcodes via ffmpeg if needed, and publishes as a native file to the channel.

5

Safe space

The discussion group is protected by multi-layered moderation:

  • Math captcha on entry
  • Goal quiz — rhythm, flexibility, strength, or "just browsing"
  • Personalized welcome DM based on the selected goal
  • 75+ anti-spam regex patterns: crypto, gambling, external links, channel promotion
  • Cooldown for new members, night mode, reputation system

Communication tone — warm, validating. Not a corporate bot, but a caring helper.

6

Market

Russia's online fitness market is 4.8B RUB across the top 25 platforms. Growing at +37% YoY. Forecast: 42B by 2032 (CAGR 20–30%).

The leader is FitStars with ~1.2B RUB revenue. The rest is a fragmented market of small players. There is no dominant Telegram fitness community.

YouTube is restricted in Russia. This creates demand for alternative fitness content delivery. Sami solves this: finds, filters, delivers — as a native file in the channel.

Trends favor the product: bodyweight home workouts are growing, community formats drive better engagement than subscriptions, and AI personalization lowers the entry barrier.

7

What's next

The Telegram channel is phase one. Validating the format, tone, and ritual.

Next step — a native app. A personal workout plan based on user data: level, goals, history. Not a video library — one workout per day, selected by algorithm. Progress tracking without gamification.

Down the road — a verified trainer marketplace and AI-powered form feedback.

The core stays the same: a ritual, not a program. Calm tone. Movement for health, not for body image.

8

Three agents

Under the hood, a system of three autonomous agents:

Strategist — runs once a day. Analyzes metrics, backlog, competitors. Produces a task packet and keywords for content search.

Community bot — lives on Railway 24/7. Receives the strategist's packet, searches videos, manages moderation, publishes content, maintains user profiles, collects analytics.

Analytics — embedded bot module. Every day at 00:30 it collects: subscribers, posts, completions, retention. Weekly reports on Sundays.
